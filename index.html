01 · The Broad Picture

A Nation Built on Winning

From the Invincibles of 1948 to the McGrath-Warne era and Pat Cummins' resurgent side, Australia has been a relentless force. The combo charts below overlay wins as bars with win-rate trend lines, while the three-line ranking chart tracks all three formats simultaneously — with key moments annotated.

Test Wins per Decade with Win Rate Trend
Dark bars = total wins. Gold line = win rate. The 2000s McGrath-Warne era is the undisputed peak.
ODI Wins per Decade with Win Rate Trend
Dark bars = total wins. Gold line = win rate. Three consecutive World Cups define the late 90s-2000s peak.
ICC Rankings — All Three Formats (2007–2024)
Gold = Test · Green = ODI · Blue = T20I. Lower rank = better. Key events annotated. The 2018 Cape Town scandal caused an unprecedented collapse across all formats.
Calendar Heatmap — Australia's Monthly Win Rate (2010–2024)
Each tile = one month of international cricket. Gold = dominant. Red = poor form. Grey = no matches. Percentage shown inside each tile. Southern summer (Oct–Mar) brings the heaviest schedule.

02 · Cricket's Greatest Rivalry

The Ashes: 140+ Years of War

Born from a satirical obituary in 1882, the Ashes has been contested 74 times. Australia leads 33 series to England's 32 with 9 drawn. The waterfall chart maps every series — gold bars climb for Australia wins, blue bars fall for England wins. Filter by location to see where each side truly dominates.

The box plot shows the spread of innings scores for top batters — revealing not just averages but consistency and range. The connected dot plot shows who dominated home and away equally.
